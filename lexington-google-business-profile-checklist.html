Checklist

Six profile checks before bigger local marketing spend

These are outside-in checks. A buyer, referral partner, or owner should be able to answer them from public pages without private dashboards or account access.

01 Category and service fit

Can a local buyer quickly tell the profile matches the service they need, not just a broad business category?

  • Primary category matches the highest-value service path.
  • Services are specific enough to help the right buyer choose.
  • Important service lines are not hidden behind generic wording.
02 Photos and proof freshness

Do photos, work examples, team cues, or location details make the business look active, local, and credible?

  • Recent photos support the services being sold.
  • Images answer trust questions before a buyer clicks away.
  • Visual proof does not feel outdated or empty.
03 Review signal and response path

Do reviews and owner responses support the buyer's next action instead of creating uncertainty?

  • Recent reviews mention services, outcomes, or buyer concerns.
  • Owner responses are current, calm, and specific.
  • The business appears to have a repeatable review request process.
04 Q&A and decision friction

Can the profile answer common buyer questions before the visitor compares another provider?

  • Important questions are answered clearly.
  • Hours, service area, appointment rules, and pricing cues are not confusing.
  • Answers push toward a useful next action.
05 Call, booking, and website handoff

Does the profile send buyers to the right next step, or does it drop them onto a generic homepage with competing paths?

  • Phone, booking, quote, or appointment options are clear.
  • Website link supports the service the profile is selling.
  • The profile and website use consistent language.
06 Tracking and follow-up recovery

Can profile-driven calls, clicks, bookings, and missed inquiries be measured and recovered?

  • Profile actions are tied to practical tracking decisions.
  • Missed calls and unbooked inquiries have a follow-up path.
  • The owner can tell whether profile traffic becomes real opportunities.
