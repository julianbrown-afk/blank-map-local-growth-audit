Review path

The audit follows the same visible path a buyer takes.

Every finding should connect to a moment where trust, clarity, proof, action, tracking, or follow-up can affect whether a buyer moves forward.

Find Local search and profile cues

Can a buyer quickly understand service area, services, profile completeness, reviews, and the next action?

Trust Website proof and reviews

Are recent reviews, photos, credentials, local proof, and service-specific examples near decision points?

Choose Service-page fit

Do key pages explain who the service is for, what happens next, what proof exists, and why to act now?

Act Call, book, quote, and follow-up

Can a buyer complete the next action and be recovered if they hesitate, miss a call, or abandon a form?

Method

Five passes, one ranked plan.

The point is to avoid generic marketing advice. Each pass turns visible friction into a decision: fix now, measure first, or ignore for the moment.

01 Map the buyer action

Identify the main call, booking, quote, consultation, or appointment path the owner wants more of.

02 Check first-screen clarity

Review what mobile and desktop buyers see before scrolling: service, location, trust, and action.

03 Connect proof to action

Look for review, work, credential, team, process, or local proof close to the decision point.

04 Find tracking and recovery gaps

Check whether calls, forms, booking clicks, missed inquiries, and follow-up can be seen and improved.

05 Rank the 30-day sequence

Order the fixes by buyer impact, effort, and whether the business should act before spending more on traffic.

Questions

About the method

Why does the audit start from public pages?

The first review starts from public pages because that is what a buyer sees before calling, booking, requesting a quote, or comparing another provider.

Do I need to send passwords?

No. The fixed-scope audit does not need website, analytics, ad account, CRM, or customer-record passwords for the first report.

What does the report prioritize?

The report prioritizes visible buyer-path fixes around mobile clarity, proof, reviews, service pages, calls to action, tracking cues, and follow-up gaps.

What is outside the audit scope?

The audit is not ad management, guaranteed SEO, guaranteed revenue, a full website rebuild, or a monthly retainer.
