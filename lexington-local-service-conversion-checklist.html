Checklist

Six buyer-path checks before bigger marketing spend

These are outside-in checks. A visitor, referral partner, or owner should be able to answer them without private dashboards or website access.

01 Mobile action clarity

Can a mobile visitor identify one primary action in the first screen: call, book, request quote, or schedule consultation?

  • Primary action is visible before scrolling.
  • Secondary links do not compete with the buying action.
  • The same action repeats after proof sections.
02 Local trust proof

Can a buyer quickly tell the business is local, active, relevant, and credible for the service they need?

  • Service area and local context are obvious.
  • Photos, team, work examples, or credentials support trust.
  • Proof appears near the decision point.
03 Reviews and reputation path

Do reviews support the action path instead of living on a separate page nobody reaches before comparing another provider?

  • Recent review proof is easy to find.
  • Review language supports the main service or buyer concern.
  • Review requests appear to be part of the process.
04 Service-page decision support

Do high-value service pages explain fit, process, proof, objections, and next step in the order a buyer needs?

  • Best services are not buried in a generic list.
  • Pages explain who the service is for.
  • Each page has a clear next action.
05 Tracking and source clarity

Can calls, forms, bookings, and quote requests be tied back to the pages or channels that influenced them?

  • Phone, form, booking, and quote actions are measurable.
  • Important clicks are not invisible.
  • Tracking supports decisions, not vanity metrics.
06 Follow-up recovery

What happens when a qualified buyer starts but does not finish a booking, form, quote, or call?

  • Unbooked inquiries have a defined follow-up path.
  • Missed calls and abandoned forms are not ignored.
  • Follow-up happens quickly enough to matter.
