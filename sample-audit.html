Finding 01

Booking action is not obvious enough on mobile

A ready buyer should not have to scan multiple sections to find the next step. The first screen should use one clear action such as "Book consultation" or "Request quote", repeated after proof and service sections.

Recommended fix: simplify the first screen and repeat the primary CTA after proof.
Finding 02

Review proof is present but not connected to the buying path

Reviews help reduce risk, but they work best when placed near the decision point. Strong review snippets, rating context, and service-specific proof should appear before the booking action.

Recommended fix: place short review proof near the consultation or quote CTA.
Finding 03

Lead tracking is too easy to miss

If calls, forms, and booking clicks are not tracked by source, it is hard to know which channels produce profitable jobs. A lightweight tracking sheet is enough to start.

Recommended fix: track calls, form fills, booking clicks, source, and outcome weekly.

Tracking notes

Calls

Log call source, service requested, booked status, and lost reason when available.

Forms

Add one hidden source field or manual source column so quote requests can be compared weekly.

Follow-up

Use one 24-hour follow-up step for unbooked inquiries before judging lead quality.

30-day action plan

  1. Clarify the highest-value service and put one primary CTA above the fold.
  2. Move proof closer to the decision point and trim low-value homepage distractions.
  3. Refresh Google profile services, photos, questions, and review request workflow.
  4. Add source tracking for calls, forms, booking clicks, and closed jobs.
